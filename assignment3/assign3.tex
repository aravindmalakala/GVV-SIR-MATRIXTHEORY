\textbf{Question}:\\
If the three points $\vec{A}\brak{x,-1}$,$\vec{B}\brak{2,1}$,$\vec{C}\brak{4,5}$ are collinear,find the value of x\\
\textbf{Solution:}\\
\begin{table}[h!]

Here $\vec{A}=\myvec{x \\-1}$, $\vec{B}=\myvec{2 \\ 1}$, and $\vec{C}=\myvec{4\\5}$

points $\vec{A}, \vec{B}, \vec{C}$ are defined to be collinear if
\begin{align}
	\label{eq:line-rank-2}
	\rank{\myvec{\vec{B}-\vec{C}& \vec{C}-\vec{A}}}=1\\
	\myvec{-2 & 4-x\\-4 & 6} \xleftrightarrow[]{R2 \leftarrow {R1*2-R2}}\\
	\myvec{-2 & 4-x \\ 0 & 2-2x}\\
\end{align}
As rank of matrix is 1
\begin{align} 
	2-2x=0\\
	\implies  x=1
\end{align}
